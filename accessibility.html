Accessibility

Designed to be usable, readable, and improving.

We want the Sheffy Adey website to be accessible to as many visitors as practical, including people using keyboards, screen readers, zoom, and reduced-motion settings.

Plain-language summary

Accessibility is an ongoing responsibility. This page explains the experience we aim to provide, the standards we use as guidance, known limitations, and how visitors can report problems.

Our accessibility goal

We aim to follow WCAG 2.2 AA where practical, build readable and keyboard-friendly pages, maintain good contrast and semantic structure, and support reduced motion where possible.

What the website should support

Keyboard navigation

Reach primary interactions without a mouse.

Responsive layouts

Readable on mobile, tablet, and desktop.

Clear headings

Logical page structure and section hierarchy.

Descriptive links

Links describe destination or action.

Alt text

Meaningful images should include text alternatives.

Visible focus states

Focused controls remain clearly visible.

Reduced-motion support

Respect visitor motion preferences where possible.

Form labels

Helpful labels and error guidance for forms.

Current limitations

Some older decorative animations may need refinement. Some third-party embeds, fonts, or remote images may be outside full control. Some pages may improve after future audits.

Feedback and support

If you experience an accessibility barrier, please send the page URL, issue description, browser/device details, assistive technology used (if relevant), and a screenshot or short recording where available.

Ongoing improvements

  • Periodic review of page templates.
  • Testing with keyboard navigation.
  • Checking color contrast.
  • Reviewing forms and dynamic interactions.
  • Adding accessible labels where needed.
